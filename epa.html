EPA Green Power Partnership

The EPA Green Power Partnership is a voluntary program led by the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA). It encourages organizations across various sectors to purchase green power as a way to reduce the environmental impacts associated with conventional electricity use.

The program supports organizational procurement of green power by providing expert advice, technical support, tools, and resources. Green power includes renewable energy sources such as wind, solar, geothermal, and low-impact hydro, which offer significant environmental benefits compared to traditional fossil fuels.

Objectives of the Green Power Partnership

  • Encourage Use of Green Power: Promote the use of renewable energy sources to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and other pollutants.
  • Provide Support and Resources: Offer technical assistance, tools, and resources to help organizations make informed decisions about green power procurement.
  • Recognize Leadership: Acknowledge and celebrate organizations that demonstrate environmental leadership through their commitment to using green power.
  • Raise Awareness: Increase awareness of the benefits of green power and the positive impacts on the environment and public health.

Benefits of Participating in the Green Power Partnership

  • Environmental Impact: Reduces greenhouse gas emissions and reliance on fossil fuels, contributing to cleaner air and a healthier environment.
  • Cost Savings: Although green power may have a higher upfront cost, many organizations find long-term savings and financial incentives through energy efficiency and renewable energy investments.
  • Enhanced Corporate Image: Demonstrates a commitment to sustainability and environmental stewardship, which can enhance a company's reputation and appeal to eco-conscious customers and stakeholders.
  • Compliance and Incentives: Helps organizations meet regulatory requirements and take advantage of federal, state, and local incentives for renewable energy adoption.

How to Get Involved

Organizations interested in participating in the Green Power Partnership can follow these steps:

  • Join the Program: Visit the EPA Green Power Partnership website to sign up and become a partner.
  • Purchase Green Power: Choose from a range of renewable energy options, including purchasing renewable energy certificates (RECs) or directly sourcing green power from energy providers.
  • Track and Report: Monitor your green power usage and report your progress to the EPA. The program provides tools and resources to help track and measure the impact of your green power purchases.
  • Engage and Educate: Share your commitment to green power with employees, customers, and the community to raise awareness and inspire others to take similar actions.

For more information about the EPA Green Power Partnership and how to get involved, visit the official EPA Green Power Partnership website.
